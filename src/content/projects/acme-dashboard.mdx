## What it does

Imposters is a party game designed for people in the same room. It supports face to face interaction rather than replacing it. At the start of each round, the app assigns roles. Every player receives the same secret word except one person, the imposter. The imposter must bluff their way through the round while the others attempt to identify them.

Players give clues one by one, discuss suspicions, and vote on who they believe is the imposter. The app manages the structure of the game by handling word assignment, timers, and eliminations. The social dynamic happens between players. The product simply keeps the game organised.

## Interaction approach

I started with a two-week research phase — interviewing 12 power users and shadowing 5 customer success calls. Three patterns emerged:

- Users checked the same 3–4 metrics daily but had to navigate multiple views to find them
- Custom report creation was powerful but poorly discoverable
- Alert thresholds were too rigid, generating notification fatigue

<PlaceholderImage aspect="4/3" caption="Research synthesis — identifying patterns across user interviews" />

## Approach

Rather than a complete redesign, I proposed a phased approach: fix the daily workflow first, then expand to custom reporting.

**Phase 1** focused on a personalized home view. Each user could pin their most-used metrics, with intelligent defaults based on role and usage patterns.

<PlaceholderImage aspect="16/9" caption="Personalized home view with pinned metrics" />

**Phase 2** introduced a simplified report builder with natural language inputs — "Show me conversion rate by channel, last 30 days" — reducing the steps from 8 to 2.

## Testing and learnings

- 40% improvement in data comprehension (measured via task completion rates)
- 3x increase in daily active usage of the dashboard
- NPS increased from 32 to 58 within the first quarter
