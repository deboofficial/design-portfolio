## Context

Wellpath wanted to enter the consumer health space with a mobile app focused on habit building. Most health apps optimize for tracking everything — we wanted to optimize for tracking the right things, consistently.

## Research

We ran a 4-week diary study with 20 participants across different health goals. The insight that shaped everything:

> People don't fail at health tracking because they lack willpower. They fail because the app asks too much of them on day one.

## Design Principles

1. **Progressive complexity** — Start with one metric. Earn the right to ask for more.
2. **Celebrate streaks, not perfection** — Missing a day doesn't break the chain.
3. **Quiet confidence** — The app should feel calm, not anxious.

## Key Decisions

The onboarding flow asks exactly one question: "What's the one thing you want to track?" Everything else is progressive disclosure.

The daily check-in is a single tap interaction. No forms, no typing, no friction. Advanced tracking is always available but never required.

## Results

- 68% day-30 retention (industry average: 25%)
- 4.7 star App Store rating
- Featured in Apple's "Apps We Love" editorial
