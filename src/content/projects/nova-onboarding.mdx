## Problem

Nova's enterprise customers took an average of 3 weeks to reach their first meaningful action in the platform. Support tickets during onboarding accounted for 40% of total volume.

## Discovery

Through customer interviews and support ticket analysis, I identified three friction points:

1. **Configuration overload** — 60+ settings presented upfront
2. **No clear path** — Users didn't know what "done" looked like
3. **Missing context** — Help content was generic, not role-specific

## Solution

A guided onboarding flow that adapts based on the user's role and use case. The system presents only the configuration options relevant to their stated goals, with sensible defaults for everything else.

Key design decisions:

- **Progressive setup** — Essential config first, everything else deferred
- **Role-based paths** — Admins, analysts, and end-users each see a tailored flow
- **Milestone celebrations** — Small moments of acknowledgment at key steps

## Results

- Time-to-first-value reduced from 3 weeks to 4 hours
- Onboarding-related support tickets decreased by 65%
- Setup completion rate increased from 54% to 89%
