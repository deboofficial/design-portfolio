## Why a Design System

FinFlow had grown to 8 product teams shipping independently. The cost was visible: inconsistent patterns, duplicated work, and a user experience that felt like 8 different products stitched together.

<PlaceholderImage aspect="16/9" caption="Visual audit — inconsistencies across the platform" />

## Audit

I cataloged every unique component, pattern, and color value across the platform. The numbers told the story:

- 47 unique button styles
- 12 different form input patterns
- 6 slightly different shades of the primary brand color

<PlaceholderImage aspect="4/3" caption="Component inventory and categorisation" />

## Strategy

Instead of building a comprehensive system upfront, I focused on the components that appeared in 80% of interfaces: buttons, inputs, cards, tables, and modals.

Each component was built with three constraints:

1. **Composable** — Components combine predictably
2. **Accessible** — WCAG AA as the minimum, AAA where practical
3. **Documented** — Every component ships with usage guidelines and examples

<PlaceholderImage aspect="16/9" caption="Core component library — buttons, inputs, and cards" />

## Adoption

Rather than mandating adoption, I embedded with each team for a sprint. Seeing the time savings firsthand converted skeptics faster than any presentation.

## Impact

- Component library reduced design-to-dev handoff time by 60%
- UI consistency score improved from 34% to 91% (measured quarterly)
- New feature development accelerated by an average of 2 weeks
